# Battlecard Format Reference

## The 6 Sections

### 1. What buyers love (Top Pros)
Clustered positive themes from review pros fields and loveTheme tags.
- 4-6 themes maximum
- Each theme needs at least 2 supporting reviews
- Show frequency count: "Ease of setup (14 reviews)"
- 2 verbatim quotes per theme, indented

### 2. What buyers complain about (Top Cons)
Clustered negative themes from review cons fields and hateTheme tags.
- 4-6 themes maximum
- Same rules as pros: 2+ reviews, verbatim quotes
- Focus on operational complaints, not wishlist features

### 3. Why buyers switch or consider switching
Specific patterns in review text signaling switching intent:

| Pattern | What to look for in review text |
|---|---|
| missing_feature | "I wish it had", "doesn't support", "we had to use another tool for" |
| pricing | "too expensive", "pricing increased", "not worth the cost" |
| performance | "slow", "crashes", "unreliable", "downtime" |
| support | "support is unresponsive", "takes days to get a reply", "no documentation" |
| integration | "doesn't connect with", "no API", "broke our workflow" |

### 4. Common objections
Drawn from 1-2 star reviews and the most frequent cons themes.
- "Frequency" = High (5+ reviews), Medium (3-4 reviews), Low (1-2 reviews)
- Suggested response should address the root concern, not dismiss it
- Keep suggested responses under 2 sentences

### 5. Discovery questions
Questions a sales rep asks to surface the pain identified in the reviews.
- All questions must be open-ended
- Valid openers: "How...", "What...", "When...", "Why...", "Tell me about...", "Walk me through..."
- Invalid: "Do you...", "Is it...", "Have you..." (yes/no questions)
- Each question should target a specific pain theme from sections 1-3

### 6. Positioning opportunity
NOT a competitive attack. NOT naming other products.
- What need does this product fail to serve?
- What buyer segment is underserved based on the review data?
- What would a better solution look like based on what buyers ask for?

---

## Quote Rules (Critical)

**Verbatim only.** Do not:
- Remove filler words ("um", "like", "basically")
- Fix grammar or spelling
- Combine two sentences into one
- Paraphrase to sound cleaner

If a quote is too long: use the most relevant sentence only, mark start/end with the exact words from the review.

**Minimum quote length:** 10 words. Single-word or fragment quotes have no credibility.

---

## What NOT to Include

- Reviewer names or company names (not available from G2 scraper)
- Claims about market share, revenue, or funding
- Anything that cannot be sourced to a specific review
- Competitor product names in the positioning opportunity section
- Claims about review authenticity (G2 verifies buyers; do not add further commentary)
